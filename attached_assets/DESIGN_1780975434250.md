## Brand & Style
The design system is engineered for the high-velocity environment of Food & Beverage (F&B) operations. It prioritizes clarity, speed of cognition, and rhythmic efficiency to reduce cognitive load for staff and managers.

The brand personality is **Professional, Systematic, and Efficient**. It utilizes a **Minimalist** design style with high-contrast elements to ensure readability under varying restaurant lighting conditions. The aesthetic leans into a "Modern Corporate" feel—clean, structured, and dependable—removing unnecessary decorative flourishes to focus entirely on task completion and data accuracy.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in a functional blue to signal trust and action. 

- **Primary Blue (#2563EB):** Used for primary actions, active states, and critical path navigation.
- **Surface & Background:** A sophisticated off-white/blue-gray background ensures the pure white (#FFFFFF) cards pop, creating clear containment for POS grids and inventory lists.
- **Semantic Colors:** Strict adherence to Green (Success/Paid), Amber (Pending/Warning), and Red (Void/Error) is required for instant status recognition in busy kitchens or checkout counters.
- **Neutral Scale:** Uses Slate grays to provide high-contrast text hierarchies against the light background.

## Typography
This design system uses **Inter** for its exceptional legibility and neutral, modern tone. 

- **Hierarchy:** Strong weight differentiation (SemiBold vs. Regular) is used to separate data labels from user input.
- **Numeric Data:** For inventory tables and POS totals, tabular lining figures should be used to ensure columns of numbers align perfectly.
- **Labels:** Small caps or increased letter spacing should be applied to `label-md` for secondary metadata (e.g., SKU numbers, timestamps) to provide contrast without increasing font size.
- **Monospace:** `JetBrains Mono` is reserved for receipt previews or technical IDs to distinguish them from editorial content.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fluid Grid** model with fixed maximum constraints for desktop to prevent line lengths from becoming unreadable in the Recipe Builder.

- **POS Grid:** Uses a flexible CSS Grid with a `minmax(120px, 1fr)` pattern for product tiles, ensuring they remain "tap-friendly" on tablets.
- **Desktop (1280px+):** 12-column grid with 24px margins. Sidebar navigation is persistent.
- **Tablet (768px - 1279px):** 8-column grid. The sidebar collapses into a rail or hamburger menu. Horizontal scrolling is permitted for wide inventory tables.
- **Mobile (<767px):** 4-column grid. Vertical stacking for forms. Inventory tables should transition into "Card List" views for better legibility.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layers** and **Ambient Shadows** to create a clear physical metaphor of stacked surfaces.

- **Level 0 (Background):** #F8FAFC. The lowest plane.
- **Level 1 (Cards/Sections):** White (#FFFFFF) with a very soft, diffused shadow (`0px 4px 12px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.05)`). This is the primary container for POS items and data tables.
- **Level 2 (Modals/Popovers):** Higher elevation shadow (`0px 12px 32px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.12)`) to indicate temporary interaction and focus.
- **Outlines:** Use a subtle 1px border (#E2E8F0) on Level 1 elements to maintain definition even when shadows are washed out by screen glare.

## Shapes
The shape language is friendly yet structured. This design system utilizes a **Rounded (2xl)** approach for its primary containers.

- **Standard Elements (Buttons, Inputs):** 0.5rem (8px) corner radius.
- **Cards & Containers:** 1rem (16px) corner radius to create a soft, modern enclosure for complex data.
- **POS Product Tiles:** 1rem (16px) radius to make them appear tactile and easy to tap.
- **Chips/Status Tags:** Fully rounded (pill) to distinguish them from interactive buttons.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Large tap targets (minimum 44px height). Primary buttons use the Primary Blue; ghost buttons use a Slate border. For "Danger" actions (e.g., Void Order), use a solid Red background.
- **POS Product Tiles:** Large cards featuring a top-aligned image and bottom-aligned price. Use `title-lg` for the price to ensure it is visible at a glance.
- **Data Tables (Inventory):** Minimalist styling with no vertical borders. Use zebra striping (Background color on even rows) for long lists. Header cells use `label-md` with a subtle bottom border.
- **Recipe Builder (Complex Forms):** Use a "Master-Detail" layout. Left side for ingredients (draggable list), right side for quantities and cost calculations. Use `rounded-lg` for input groups.
- **Chips:** Used for order status (e.g., "Dine-in", "Takeaway"). Use low-saturation background tints with high-saturation text of the same hue.
- **Input Fields:** 1px Slate-200 border, turning Blue-600 on focus. Labels should always be visible (not floating) for maximum accessibility.
